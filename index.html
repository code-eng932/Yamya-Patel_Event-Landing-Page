The Impact of Deforestation

Biodiversity Loss

Forests are home to over 80% of the world's terrestrial biodiversity. Deforestation leads to habitat destruction and threatens countless species with extinction.

Climate Change

Trees absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen. Deforestation not only reduces this carbon sink capacity but also releases stored carbon back into the atmosphere.

Water Cycle Disruption

Forests regulate water cycles by absorbing rainfall and releasing water vapor into the atmosphere. Deforestation leads to soil erosion, flooding, and water pollution.
